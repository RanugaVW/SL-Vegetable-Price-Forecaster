================================================================================
Weekly Producer Vegetable Prices - Missing Data Analysis Report
================================================================================

This report details the missing value (blank price) percentages at both stages 
of the dataset preparation process.

1. RAW EXCEL DATASET: 'weekly_producer_vegetable_all_location(2008-2024).xlsx'
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Looking strictly at the raw dataset before any location or vegetable filtering:
- Total valid price cells (W1 to W52 for all documented rows): 114,036
- Missing (blank) price cells: 42,260
- Data Missing Percentage: 37.06%

*Note: The raw spreadsheet only lists a vegetable for a particular location if 
there was actually data recorded for it during that year. It inherently skips 
many potential location/vegetable pairings that had no activity.


2. GENERATED LONG-FORMAT CSV (FILTERED & EXPANDED)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Target parameters: 
- Time period: 2013-w1 to 2019-w52 (7 years, 364 weeks)
- Locations: 14 target locations
- Vegetables: 12 target vegetables

To ensure a continuous and robust time-series structure, a fully dense mathematical 
grid was generated mapping *every single week* to *every location* and *every vegetable*. 
Prices were merged in only where they existed historically.
- Total structural combinations (364 weeks * 14 locations * 12 vegetables): 61,152 rows
- Missing (blank) prices over this theoretically perfect 7-year grid: 47,563 rows
- Valid prices historically found: 13,589 rows
- Data Missing Percentage: 77.78%

*Conclusion: The significantly higher 77.78% sparsity in the final output is 
mostly because the script systematically generated empty structural placeholders 
for non-existent/unreported historic market movements to ensure a consistent size 
(61,152) across every time step in the time-series model.
